# THE UGLY DUCKLING - Premium Children's Picture Book Design System

## Page Format
- **Dimensions**: 11" × 8.5" (792 × 612 pt) — Classic landscape picture book
- **Bleed**: Full page illustrations (0.125" bleed simulated via image)
- **Safe text margin**: 0.6" from all edges

## Visual Style Direction
**Art Direction**: "Modern premium picture book" — the warmth of Beatrix Potter + the emotional expressiveness of Pixar/Dreamworks storybook art + the painterly softness of contemporary bestsellers (e.g. "The Day the Crayons Quit", "The Invisible Boy", "Last Stop on Market Street").

- **Medium feel**: Soft watercolor washes + crisp digital details + subtle paper texture
- **Lighting**: Always warm, golden hour or gentle morning light. Never harsh. Soft rim lighting on characters.
- **Color Palette** (hex for reference in code):
  - Primary warm cream: #FDF8F0 (text backgrounds)
  - Sky & water: #A8D5E5 → #E8F4F8 → #F5E6D3
  - Foliage: #3D6B4F (deep forest), #5C8C6E (sage), #8FB996 (soft mint)
  - Accent warm: #E07A5F (coral beak), #F4A261 (golden orange), #E8C547 (soft gold)
  - Character grey duckling: #8B8B8B down → pure #F8F8F8 as swan
  - Text body: #2C3E50 (warm dark slate)
  - Titles: #1E3A3A (deep teal forest)

## Typography System
- **Title font (cover + page titles)**: Brush Script (BRUSHSCI.TTF) or Lucida Calligraphy for elegant handwritten premium feel. Large 42-56pt, generous tracking.
- **Body text**: Georgia (georgia.ttf) 13-15pt for readability, excellent screen + print. Line height 1.35.
- **Accent / moral text**: Georgia-Italic or small caps.
- **Page numbers**: Georgia 10pt, centered, with delicate feather motif.

## Character Bible (for consistency across generations)
**The Duckling / Young Swan (hero)**:
- Duckling stage: Larger body, noticeably longer neck than siblings, big webbed feet, fluffy medium-grey down, large dark brown eyes with soft white highlight (soulful, slightly melancholic but kind expression)
- Transition: Feathers gradually lighten
- Swan stage: Pure white plumage, long elegant S-neck, warm orange-yellow beak, confident yet gentle eyes, graceful posture

**Mother Duck**:
- Warm cream breast, soft brown back/wings, bright orange beak with slight smile, wise kind eyes with lashes, protective wing posture, soft head feathers

**Yellow Ducklings (siblings)**:
- Roundest fluffiest bodies, tiny wings, bright lemon-yellow, tiny orange beaks, playful bouncy energy

**The Swans**:
- Adults: Tall, brilliant white, long necks in perfect curves, orange beaks with black tips sometimes, serene expressions

## Layout Principles (Premium)
- Art is the hero — 80-90% of page
- Text lives in carefully chosen "quiet zones" of the illustration (sky, calm water, soft meadow)
- Elegant semi-transparent cream rounded panels (opacity 88-94%) with 12-18pt inner padding when text must overlay busy art
- Generous "air" and breathing room around text blocks
- Subtle drop shadow or very soft outer glow on text for legibility over complex art
- Never crowd characters — text respects their emotional space
- Consistent 0.5" inner margin gutter feeling even in landscape

## Page-by-Page Layout Notes
1. **Cover**: Title top third in elegant script, subtitle smaller below, art dominant, tiny author line at very bottom
2. **Hatching**: Text top left or bottom in soft panel. Nest scene intimate
3. **Teasing**: Text bottom third in larger panel. Emotional focus on the hurt duckling
4. **Winter**: Text left or right panel. Vast lonely landscape, duckling small but not lost
5. **Reflection**: Text top or as caption under swan. Magical moment, light and awe
6. **Ending**: Text in beautiful bottom panel or as two short stanzas. Celebration scene

## Finishing Touches for Pro Quality
- Delicate corner leaf/flower ornaments drawn in reportlab (subtle)
- Very faint paper texture overlay on every page (light noise or generated texture)
- Consistent small header ornament (tiny flying duck silhouette or reed) + book title in 7pt
- Page numbers with "·" or "— " elegant separators
- Final PDF: PDF/X-4 or high quality settings, embedded fonts, CMYK friendly but RGB for screen too

This book must feel like a $24.99 heirloom picture book from a top publisher.
